# 20 July 2026: H.264 to fMP4 LL-HLS at 4K and 8K

This qualification sends realtime H.264 and AAC MPEG-TS from a DAW host to
`av-contrib` over SRT. The contributor demultiplexes the stream, packages CMAF
fMP4, and publishes canonical media through both relay parents. A separate
reader fetches the edge LL-HLS playlist and performs strict FFmpeg decode.

The machine record is
[`20260720T034744Z-h264-fmp4-llhls-4k-8k.json`](evidence/20260720T034744Z-h264-fmp4-llhls-4k-8k.json).
Retained playlists and init evidence are under
`target/gcp-qualification/video-h264-fmp4-llhls/20260720`.

## Result

| Workload | Realtime source rate | Publish window | fMP4 parts | Media bytes | Strict decode | Result |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- | --- |
| 3840 x 2160 H.264 + AAC | 39.411 Mbit/s | 50 s | 1,294 | 207,562,258 | 286 frames in 10.01 s at 1.21x | pass |
| 7680 x 4320 H.264 + AAC | 125.531 Mbit/s | 50 s | 1,476 | 748,595,513 | 77 frames in 3 s at 0.643x | pass |

Both workloads produced an init segment with `avc1` and `mp4a`, readable LL-HLS
parts and completed segments, and strict H.264 plus AAC decode with exit status
zero. Contributor publish errors, relay errors, MPEG-TS continuity errors, and
service restarts were zero.

The 4K fixture is a ten-second 3840 x 2160 rendition derived from a real
Lori Asha source. The 8K fixture is a 7680 x 4320 Level 6.2 upscale of that
source. It is valid transport and packaging stress at about 125.5 Mbit/s, not
a native-8K visual-quality claim. The two-vCPU reader decoded it below realtime;
that is a reader decode limit, not evidence of edge backlog.

## Defects found and fixed

The first 4K run exposed an fMP4 epoch bug. A video access unit could create a
new init segment without carrying forward the known AAC signature. The result
declared video-only initialization while later fragments still referenced the
audio track. `av-contrib` now retains the known audio signature, and a
regression test requires `avc1`, `mp4a`, and two `trex` entries.

The video timestamps also exposed playlist-duration assumptions that 5 ms Opus
did not reveal. `av-mesh` now records observed part duration, sums observed
parts for `EXTINF`, and derives `TARGETDURATION` from the maximum completed
segment rather than multiplying a single outlier part duration.

Final 4K metadata used `TARGETDURATION:8`, `PART-TARGET:0.167`, and 14.144 s
hold-back. Final 8K metadata used `TARGETDURATION:7`, `PART-TARGET:0.067`, and
13.268 s hold-back. Most video parts were 33 or 67 ms, matching the source
frame cadence.

## Private topology

All media stayed within one `europe-west2-c` private subnet:

```text
DAW 10.84.10.4 -> contributor 10.84.10.5
  -> relay parents 10.84.10.7 and 10.84.10.8
  -> edge 10.84.10.6 -> reader 10.84.10.9
```

IAP was used only for orchestration, fixture transfer, and evidence retrieval.
There was no inter-region media path or paid inter-region test traffic.

## Remaining gates

This proves the contributor H.264-to-fMP4 path and LL-HLS server at one viewer.
It does not prove browser hardware decode, native 8K image quality, multi-viewer
video capacity, adaptive rendition switching, or a long-duration video soak.
